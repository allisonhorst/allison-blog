Walking through the door

I am always happy to see this illustration used to represent of the welcoming and encouraging #rstats community on twitter. When I drew it, my view was much smaller: I wanted to visualize how Dr. Julie Lowndes (Twitter: @juliesquid, website: https://jules32.github.io/) had gently encouraged me to join twitter for the R community, and the amazement I felt upon opening the door. I was late to the party - finally dipping my toes into the #rstats twitter water in 2018. Two years later, it’s now my go-to place for:

  • Staying up-to-date on what’s new in R
  • Meeting and communicating with new friends and collaborators
  • Learning new ideas and approaches for teaching stats and data science
  • Sharing my course materials and artwork
  • Listening to, and learning from, a diverse range of voices in R/data science/environmental science

The rumors are true, people. It’s the place to be if you’re learning, using, or teaching R.

R is better together

I’ve been teaching stats & data analysis in R at the Bren School of Environmental Science and Management since 2012. But using and teaching R is very different from being a happy R user and teacher.

Frankly, I was a lonely R user flailing around without a home base, and I didn’t even realize how much of a bummer that was.

In 2017, Julie serendipitously moved in to a cabin just one short down the road from me. Do you know what happens when you finally get to hang out with someone who loves using and teaching R as much as you do? You hang out all the time and never shut up about it.

It’s obnoxious.

We talk about using R on every hike.

We talk about teaching R over bonfires.

We talk about building inclusive R communities while romping with our dogs, Teddy and Khora, in the riverbed.

We talk about R frustrations while eating pizza.

We talk about R at SB R-Ladies (https://twitter.com/RLadiesSB), UCSB TidyTuesdays, and Eco-Data-Science.

It’s so obnoxious.
